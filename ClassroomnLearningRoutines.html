Understanding Routines

What are Routines?

Routines are sequences of actions regularly followed, providing structure to time and interactions. They are the established habits and expectations that bring order and efficiency to the classroom, fostering predictability crucial for academic and social thriving.

Instructional Routines

These are specific, repeatable designs for learning that structure teacher-student interaction around academic content. They aim to foster critical thinking, promote agency, and manage cognitive load for deeper processing.

Effective Implementation Strategies

General Principles

  1. Reflect & Identify: Define desired skills, habits, and smooth transitions.
  2. Explicitly Teach & Model: Explain the "why" and demonstrate steps (positive/negative examples).
  3. Guided & Independent Practice: Allow ample time for students to internalize.
  4. Visual Aids & Reminders: Display concise, positive rules and routine steps.
  5. Consistency & Reinforcement: Practice diligently; use behavior-specific praise and gentle redirection.
  6. Student Involvement & Feedback: Empower students in development and modification for buy-in.

Key Insight: Explaining the rationale ("why") is crucial for intrinsic motivation and deeper understanding, moving beyond surface-level compliance.

üåç Tailoring for Diverse Learners

Predictability benefits all, especially diverse learners. Differentiate by offering choice in engagement (written, verbal, artistic). Ensure routines are universally inclusive.

üîÑ Monitoring & Adapting Over Time
